The Real Bottleneck

Africa does not just need more farmers. It needs processors operating at scale.

Many processors have machinery, buyers, and ambition — but still operate below capacity because supply is fragmented, working capital is limited, crop quality is inconsistent, and raw goods are expensive to move.

01

Processors lack reliable supply

Farmers are fragmented and processors struggle to source predictable volumes at the right quality.

02

Working capital is constrained

Processors often need capital to buy inventory before they can fulfill buyer orders.

03

Raw crops are costly to move

Bulky, wet, or perishable crops lose value before they reach industrial facilities.

04

Data is missing

Investors and lenders lack the visibility needed to finance production with confidence.

The AVRAURA Model

Start with processors. Then build the infrastructure around them.

We are not building another farmer marketplace. AVRAURA begins with the processor’s pain: “What keeps your factory from running at full capacity?”

  • Map underutilized processors and their bottlenecks.
  • Match confirmed buyer demand to processor capacity.
  • Coordinate supply from farmer clusters and aggregators.
  • Unlock working capital through demand-backed financing.
  • Deploy micro-processing nodes where logistics data proves the need.

Micro-Infrastructure

What a micro-processing node looks like.

A node is a small, modular unit placed near farmer clusters. It performs the first transformation step before long-distance transport.

AVRAURA NODE

Cocoa: ferment → dry → grade → trace

Immediate Execution Plan

The first product is not software. It is proof of processor demand.

Week 1–2

Interview 25 processors

Ask what prevents 100% utilization: supply, finance, quality, logistics, buyers, or equipment.

Week 3–4

Pick one corridor

Choose one crop and one region where bottlenecks repeat across processors.

Month 2

Coordinate first transaction

Match processor demand with supply and document every failure point.

Month 3–6

Finance or deploy first node

If logistics or quality is the bottleneck, pilot one small pre-processing node with a processor partner.
