The Problem

Crops often lose value before they reach the processor.

In many African agricultural value chains, crops are harvested and moved before they are properly dried, sorted, stored, fermented, or prepared. By the time they reach a processing facility, quality has dropped and everyone earns less.

See The Solution →
1

Farmers sell too early

Farmers often sell crops quickly because they lack tools, storage, or guaranteed buyers.

2

Processors receive poor inputs

Wet, damaged, unsorted, or inconsistent crops make processing harder and reduce output quality.

3

Buyers pay less

When quality is low or inconsistent, processors and buyers cannot pay premium prices.

Our Solution

Fix the quality problem at the source.

Avrauca supports simple farm-gate preparation before crops move down the supply chain. This can include drying beds, fermentation boxes, sorting, cleaning, better storage, and links to processors who are ready to buy higher-quality inputs.

How It Works

We connect the people who need each other most.

1. Start with one crop

We focus on crops where quality drops quickly before processing, like cocoa, cassava, cashews, shea, sesame, or coffee.

2. Improve farm-gate preparation

We help farmers prepare crops better before transport using low-cost tools and simple quality standards.

3. Connect to processors

We send processors better raw materials so they lose less, produce more, and make better products.

4. Bring in buyers and financing

Once quality and demand are proven, we help connect buyers and financing to support larger production.

Farmers win. Processors win. Buyers win.

Farmers can earn more from better quality crops. Processors get cleaner and more reliable inputs. Buyers receive better products. Avrauca helps make that chain work from the farm gate to the final sale.
